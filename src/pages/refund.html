Refund Policy

Last updated: June 12, 2026

Paddle acts as the authorised reseller for Bridge subscriptions. All transactions for Bridge are processed by Paddle. This policy describes when you may be entitled to a refund and how to request one. If local consumer protection laws provide additional rights, the highest level of protection applies.

7-Day Free Trial

All new accounts receive a 7-day free trial. No credit card required. If you cancel during the trial, you will not be charged.

14-Day Discretionary Refund

Paddle may, at its discretion, issue a refund for subscription charges if a request is submitted within 14 days of the transaction date. Submission within this period does not guarantee a refund. All requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis. To request a refund, contact Paddle's buyer support at https://paddle.net or use the "View receipt" or "Manage subscription" link in your transaction confirmation email.

Statutory Withdrawal Rights

If you are a consumer in the European Union, EEA, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom, you have a 14-day statutory right to withdraw from the first payment under a subscription contract and receive a full refund. This right applies to the first payment only, not to subsequent recurring payments. If your subscription includes a free trial, you have a new 14-day withdrawal period starting when the free trial ends.

Consumers in Turkey, Israel, Canada, South Korea, Brazil, and China may have statutory withdrawal or cancellation rights ranging from 5 to 14 days depending on local law. Consumers in Singapore have a 5-day cancellation right.

To exercise a statutory withdrawal right, request a refund via the methods described above within the applicable period.

Cancellation

You may cancel your Bridge subscription at any time from the Bridge dashboard or by using the "Manage subscription" link in your transaction confirmation email. Access continues until the end of the current billing period. No partial refunds are issued for cancellations made mid-cycle, except where required by applicable law.

Chargebacks and Disputes

We encourage you to contact Paddle directly before initiating a chargeback or dispute with your bank or card issuer. If you do initiate a chargeback, access to Bridge may be temporarily suspended while the matter is reviewed. This does not affect your lawful rights under card-scheme or consumer-protection rules.

Technical Issues

Updates to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The version in effect at the time of your transaction governs that transaction.
